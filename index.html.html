Our
Story

Trey and Kimi, Myanmar refugees,
started a school in Danau Kota,
Setapak, with support from Hope
Evangelical Free Church. Backed by
UNHCR and a RM12,000 grant, the
school opened in 2008 with 20
students. As it grew, the school
moved to a shop-office lot. When
Trey and Kimi moved to the US,
Hope EFC took over administration.
Communitas Wangsa Maju Berhad,
an NGO, ensured accountability.
After 14 years, NH operates classes
for ages 5 to 18,with 217 students
and 8 full-time teachers, 7 of
whom are refugees. Generous
donations sustain NH, and
the goal is to better equip
the school for serving
refugee students
and families in the future.

Our
Story

Trey and Kimi, Myanmar refugees, started a school in Danau Kota, Setapak, with support from Hope Evangelical Free Church. Backed by UNHCR and a RM12,000 grant, the school opened in 2008 with 20 students. As it grew, the school moved to a shop-office lot. When Trey and Kimi moved to the US, Hope EFC took over administration. Communitas Wangsa Maju Berhad, an NGO, ensured accountability.After 14 years, NH operates classes for ages 5 to 18,with 217 students and 8 full-time teachers, 7 of whom are refugees. Generous donations sustain NH, and the goal is to better equip the school for serving refugee students and families in the future.
